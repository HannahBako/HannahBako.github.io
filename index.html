Hannah K. Bako

Data Visualization, Creativity, Design, Human Computer Interaction

I'm a PhD student advised by Dr. Zhicheng (Leo) Liu at the Computer Science Department at the University of Maryland, College Park. My research lies at the intersection of Data visualization,and Human Computer Interaction where I take a human-centered approach to understanding how designers create data visualizations and using that knowledge to support the development of novel visualization authoring tools. I previously worked with Dr. Leilani Battle in the BAttle Data (BAD) Lab where I worked on building tools to support visualization programming and design in D3.

I have a Masters in Software engineering from Stevens institute of Technology and a Bachelors degree in Computer Information Systems from Babcock University.
